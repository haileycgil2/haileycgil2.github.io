Hailey Gil



Hailey Gil moved from Sacramento to Eagle Rock in 2016 to pursue her undergraduate degree in Urban & Environmental Policy at Occidental College. At Oxy she works to encourage her peers to develop responsible relationships with the surrounding community by supporting small local businesses, and volunteering for local nonprofits. She is also involved in the Christian community at Oxy, where she participates in a small group bible study focusing on the intersection of faith and social justice.

Hailey currently works as the Community Outreach Coordinator for Reach Our Community Kids (ROCK) Community Center, on Townsend Avenue and Yosemite Drive. Previously, she interned at the Southern California Association for Nonprofit Housing (SCANPH) to advocate for permanent supportive housing, and at Strategic Actions for a Just Economy (SAJE) to advocate for tenant’s rights.

Hailey loves spending time with friends, volunteering in the community, taking trips to the beach, and watching movies. In the future, she dreams of getting master’s degrees in urban planning and public policy so that she can better serve her community.
